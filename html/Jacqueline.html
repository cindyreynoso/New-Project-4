Which five countries have the highest/lowest overall percent anxiety diagnosed compared to UN HDI? Is there a correlation?

The United Nations Human Development Index (HDI) is a summary measure of average human achievement based on: a long healthy life, being knowledgable, and having a decent standard of living.

The assumption here is that, if a country's HDI is high (meaning its citizens' needs are taken care of) then mental health issues might decrease.

However it appears the opposite is true. Countries with high HDI have higher mental health diagnosis rates. There are a few reasons why this may be caused.

1. With a low HDI, lack of knowledge also means lack of mental health services

2. Countries with higher HDI also tend to have less 'cultural taboo' against mental health issues

Do certain regions have higher rates of specific mental health diagnoses?

Put simply, which regions have high and low rates of each disorder? Economy, government, culture, and religion all have different impacts on people, including their mental health.

When looking at the previous charts, North America has some of the highest diagnoses of mental health issues over every other region. It also has a high HDI. Right below it, Central America has low mental health diagnosis and lower average HDIs. Perhaps Central American culture explains the difference.

As for alcohol use rates, Europe has the highest above any other region. European drinking culture may explain this (wine/pub cultures). SubSaharan Africa has the lowest, along with the Middle East. As most of the Middle East practices Islam, it is not unusual that alcohol use (which is considered haram or forbidden) is low. However, SubSaharan Africa with a much lower Muslim population has lower alcohol use disorder rates than the Middle East. What causes this is under speculation as the Middle East again has a higher HDI on average than SubSaharan Africa.

Perhaps SubSaharan Africa has such low rates across every chart is because it has a low HDI. Much of SubSaharan Africa are part of industrializing countries, which may not have the means more industrialized countries do to focus on mental health. Or there is a lack of research and resources for mental health in these areas.
